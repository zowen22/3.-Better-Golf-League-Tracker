Does this handle your league?

The short answer: almost certainly. Here's exactly what's supported, so you don't have to guess.

Scoring Formats

Pick one per season in League Settings — you're not locked in, and each format keeps its own history.

  • Match Play — the classic hole-by-hole win/loss/tie between two teams, with points per hole plus an overall match point.
  • Stableford — points per hole based on net score relative to par (Eagle = 4, Birdie = 3, Par = 2, Bogey = 1, Double-or-worse = 0), no head-to-head opponent needed.
  • Best Ball — each team's lower of its two net scores counts per hole, compared against the opposing team.
  • Team Totals — both teammates' net scores are added together per hole and compared against the opposing team's total.
  • Classical Stroke Play — a field-wide format (no team-vs-team matchup at all): every player earns points off their own score relative to par, compared across the whole league.

Handicaps

  • Automatic handicap index calculation from entered scores, with full per-round history.
  • Choose the differential method per league: simple par-based (gross − par) or full WHS-style (course slope/rating).
  • Configurable handicap window, number of scores dropped, handicap percentage applied, and a maximum handicap index cap.
  • Net or gross scoring — toggle whether handicap strokes apply at all.
  • Starting handicaps can be seeded for a new season in one step from the Season Setup wizard.

Format Flexibility

  • 9-hole or 18-hole rounds.
  • Single course, or multiple courses/tees across a season.
  • Configurable rules for absences and substitutes — including whether a sub'd-in score can still win the overall match point.
  • Optional max-score-per-hole cap (warn or hard-cap), if your league plays "pick up at double bogey" style rules.

Beyond the Scorecard

  • Skins — including handicap-tiered flights, gross or net.
  • Playoffs and a full standings/points system.
  • Contests (closest-to-pin, long drive, and recurring weekly contests) and dues/buy-in tracking.
  • Self-reporting for players entering their own scores, with admin approval.
  • Stats, records, and a Hall of Fame, plus a member forum.
